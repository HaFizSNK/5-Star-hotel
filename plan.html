Plan Your Trip

“Careful planning is at the heart of ensuring that World Heritage sites benefit from the high profile that comes with their global status, through collaboration between the private sector, local communities and site managers,” says Giulia Carbone, Deputy Director of IUCN’s Global Business and Biodiversity Programme. “If managed sustainably, World Heritage sites can give tourists the opportunity to visit some of the most beautiful places that the world has to offer while benefitting the natural environment at the same time.”

Top -Tips:

  • Travel light: limit the packaging you bring with you. This will become waste in your holiday destination.
  • Wildlife watching can be an incredible experience… but don't disturb wildlife, for your own safety and theirs!
  • Maintain a relationship with your new friends in the destination, become a member of local conservation organizations.
  • Pick nature-friendly accommodation: ask hotels if they are truly eco, for example do they have an environmental policy?
  • Many wild plants and animals are in great danger … you can contribute to protecting them by avoiding buying souvenirs made from Endangered species
